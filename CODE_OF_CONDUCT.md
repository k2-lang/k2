# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

The k2 project is a place to design and build a programming language together.
We want taking part — asking a question, filing a bug, sending a patch, or
reviewing one — to be a good experience for everyone, regardless of experience
level, background, or identity.

## The short version

- **Be technical, not personal.** Critique code and ideas, never people.
- **Assume good faith.** Most disagreements are misunderstandings; ask before
  you accuse.
- **Be precise and kind.** A clear, friendly explanation helps more than a
  terse "this is wrong."
- **Respect the maintainers' time.** Search first, give a reproduction, and
  accept that "no" is a legitimate answer to a feature request.

## Not acceptable

Harassment, insults, discriminatory language, sustained disruption of
discussions, publishing others' private information, and any conduct that would
reasonably make someone feel unsafe taking part. This applies in the issue
tracker, pull requests, commit messages, and any other project space.

## Reporting

If you see behavior that violates this code — or that simply makes the project
worse to be part of — report it privately to the maintainers at
**conduct@k2-lang.org**. Reports are handled confidentially. Maintainers who do
not act in good faith on a report may themselves face consequences.

Maintainers may warn, temporarily restrict, or permanently ban anyone whose
behavior they judge to be inappropriate, threatening, or harmful.
